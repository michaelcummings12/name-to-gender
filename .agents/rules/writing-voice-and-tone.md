All user-facing copy (marketing pages, emails, in-app strings, metadata, CTAs) follows one voice: calm, confident, plain. State the benefit and let it land. When writing or editing any copy, apply these rules.

### No em dashes

Never use `—` (em dash) in copy. It reads as machine-written. Replace it with the punctuation a person would actually use:

- **Period** when the two halves are full thoughts
- **Comma** when one half depends on the other
- **Colon** when the second half explains or lists

This applies to copy only. Standalone `—` as a "no value" marker in data tables is fine, and code comments are out of scope.

### Confidence, not contempt

State a benefit calmly. Never oversell with absolutes or drama. Never name or mock the enemy.

### Calm CTAs

Short, lowercase-feeling verbs. No shouting, no exclamation points.

### Clean sentences

Write parallel, complete sentences. Don't stack fragments and dashes for drama.
